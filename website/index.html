Observe, plan, act, verify

Autonomy with a brake pedal.

ArgosFS treats self-driving storage as a safety-constrained control loop. It records observations, explains candidate actions, checks recoverability, executes bounded maintenance, and verifies the result before the next round.

Explore the research model
01

Observe

Probe capacity, latency, SMART coverage, workload heat, and failure domains.

02

Score drain, scrub, rebalance, and observe actions with explicit rejection reasons.

03

Act

Rewrite shards across diverse disks with latency-throttled background budgets.

04

Verify

Run fsck checks and downgrade automation when verification fails.

Why it exists

Built for the messy middle between filesystem research and real machines.

Rootfs-aware data placement

Boot-critical paths and explicit xattrs steer placement toward hot, diverse failure domains.

Health transparency

SMART reports expose refresh time, parser type, observed fields, missing fields, and stale data.

Compatibility surface

FUSE operations cover rename flags, sticky directories, ACL xattrs, non-UTF-8 names, and fsync.

Reproducible evaluation

Experiment scripts generate raw JSONL/CSV, processed summaries, tables, and run manifests.
